Understand Your Marital Property

The first and most important step is understanding which assets are considered marital property and which are separate property. This distinction is crucial in India, where the court often considers contributions, ownership, and duration of the asset when deciding divisions.

  • Marital property: Assets acquired during marriage, including joint bank accounts, property purchased together, vehicles, and investments.
  • Separate property: Assets owned before marriage, gifts from family, or inheritances that remain in your name.

Example: If you bought a house before marriage with your personal savings, it typically counts as separate property. But if you renovated it using joint funds, the renovation portion could be considered marital property.

Consider a Postnuptial Agreement

Postnuptial Agreements in India

In India, while prenuptial agreements are not very common, a postnuptial agreement can still be drafted to protect assets after marriage.

  • Clearly defines which assets remain individual property
  • Helps avoid disputes over finances during divorce
  • Must be drafted legally, signed voluntarily, and witnessed to be enforceable

Example: A postnuptial agreement can state that a family inheritance or a separately purchased property will remain with one spouse, even in the event of a divorce.

Retirement Savings and Investments

Retirement savings and investments can be complicated in divorce cases. While India does not split PF accounts like some countries, other investments may be considered marital assets.

Key Steps:

  • Seek advice from a financial planner or tax consultant if necessary

Example: Contributions made individually to mutual funds may remain yours, but gains on jointly invested funds may be subject to division.
